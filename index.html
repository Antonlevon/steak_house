"We built the room around the fire, not the other way around."

Cascade & Coal began as a single hearth hauled into a former machine shop on Capitol Hill. The brick still holds the char. We cook over Cascade alderwood and oak coal — no gas, no shortcuts — and let the smoke do the seasoning it has always done.

Our beef is dry-aged on the bone for forty days in a cabinet you can see from the bar. The result is darker, mineral, unmistakably itself. Around it: Northwest oysters, cast-iron sides, and a whiskey program that reads like a diary. Come hungry, stay late.
